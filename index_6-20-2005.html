Thomas Schaefer

I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Physics at North Carolina State University. I am a member of the Nuclear Theory Group at North Carolina State, and a fellow at the RIKEN-BNL Research Center.


I am interested in QCD, the theory of quarks and gluons. I study the phase structure of QCD, the behavior of matter under extreme conditions, topological phenomena in gauge theories and hadronic physics. I have studied these problems using a variety of methods, including perturabtive QCD, instanton calculations, effective field theory methods, and numerical approaches.
