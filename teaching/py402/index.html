Quantum Mechanics II (PY 402)

PROF. T. SCHAEFER



Physics 401/402 (Quantum Mechanics) provides an introduction to quantum mechanics intended for undergraduate students in physics or engineering. Part II (PY402) covers perturbation theory (time independent and time dependent), applications to atomic physics, scattering theory, and a brief introduction to relativistic quantum mechanics.

  5. Textbook
    Quantum Mechanics by Phillip J. E. Peebles, Princeton Univeristy Press. Supplemental Material:
    • Introduction to Quantum Mechanics, by David Griffiths, Benjamin Cummings.
      A popular undergraduate text book. Not as well written as Peebles, but structure closer to typical introductory physics books.
    • Modern Quantum Mechanics, by J. J. Sakurai, Addison Wesley.
      Not a good text book, but very useful supplement for both undergraduate and graduate students.
    • Principles of Quantum Mechanics, by R. Shankar, Springer.
      A little more challenging, and frequently used as a graduate textbook. Very well written and good for self study.
